**01 About This Year's Theme** 

Squad Hackathon 3.0 goes beyond building to building with purpose. The theme "Smart Systems: The Intelligent Economy" is a call to the boldest, most curious minds in Nigerian universities to engineer solutions that don't just process payments, but reshape how communities interact with money. 

The challenges are grounded in a single belief: financial inclusion is not a charity problem. It is a technology problem. And you have the tools to solve it. 

Every challenge requires integration with Squad's API stack. Every solution must demonstrate real intelligence whether through Al automation, predictive data, or bold financial innovation. Building a demo is not enough. Build something that works for the people who need it most. 

---

**02 Your Guiding Four Pillars** 

**The Four Pillars Every Solution Must Address** All the challenges are evaluated against the Squad Hackathon 3.0 four core innovation pillars. Your solution does not need to max out every pillar, but it must meaningfully engage with the ones most relevant to your challenge. 

* 
**Al Automation:** Design Al-powered workflows that automate the future of financial technology. 


* 
**Use of Data:** Create predictive, data driven solutions that protect communities and keep transactions secure in real time. 


* 
**Squad APIs:** Power smart financial services that scale with our powerful API stack. 


* 
**Financial Innovation:** Create bold solutions that transform how money moves.

**03 The Challenges and How You Will Be Judged** 

**Challenge 01** 

**The Problem Statement: "Proof of Life"** Build an Al solution that helps people, businesses, or institutions verify, trust, or authenticate something in a context where fraud or opacity is common. 

Fake exam results. Ghost workers on government payrolls. Counterfeit products in supply chains. Unverified vendors collecting payments. Misinformation spreading as fact. 

The Al must do the verification or trust-scoring work, forcing teams into computer vision, NLP, or anomaly detection territory. This is not a chat interface challenge. 

**Example Use Cases (Choose One or Propose Your Own)** Your solution does not need to cover all of these. Pick the domain where your team can build the most technically rigorous solution: 

| Domain | Verification Challenge |
| --- | --- |
| **Government & HR** | Detect ghost workers on payrolls using biometric signals, attendance patterns, or anomaly detection across payroll datasets 

 |
| **Education** | Verify the authenticity of academic certificates, transcripts, or exam results using document analysis and institutional data 

 |
| **Supply Chain** | Identify counterfeit products or unverified vendors in a procurement or retail supply chain using image recognition or metadata analysis 

 |
| **Financial Services** | Score the trustworthiness of an individual, business, or transaction using behavioural signals, document verification, or network analysis 

 |
| **Media & Information** | Detect fake news, manipulated images, or synthetic media (deepfakes) using NLP, computer vision, or source credibility modelling 

 |
| **Healthcare** | Verify the authenticity of medical credentials, prescriptions, or diagnostic results to prevent fraud in insurance or telemedicine 

 |

**Your Mission** You are a technical team commissioned by a government agency, financial institution, or private sector organisation to build an Al-powered verification system that addresses a real, documented fraud or trust problem. Your mission is to design, build, and demonstrate a system that: 

* Identifies a specific, real-world context where verification failures cause measurable harm 


* Uses Al, computer vision, NLP, anomaly detection, or a combination as the core verification or trust-scoring engine 


* Produces a clear, interpretable output: a trust score, a pass/fail verdict, a risk flag, or an anomaly alert 


* Handles edge cases: what happens when data is incomplete, forged, or adversarial? 


* Integrates Squad API for payment workflows 


* Is deployable at scale and not just a proof of concept that works on 10 clean examples 



Your solution must demonstrate technical depth. A working model, live demo, or interactive prototype will significantly strengthen your score. Slide decks alone will not be enough to win. 

**How You Will Be Judged** Teams will be evaluated by a panel of technical and industry judges across six criteria. Scores are weighted and combined to produce a final ranking. 

| Criteria | Weight | Summary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1. Al Technical Depth** | 30% | Quality and correctness of the AI/ML core - model choice, training approach, and output reliability 

 |
| **2. Squad API Integration** | 20% | Meaningful, technically correct use of Squad API as a functional part of the verification workflow 

 |
| **3. Problem Relevance & Domain Insight** | 15% | Evidence that the team understands the real-world fraud context they are addressing 

 |
| **4. Solution Design & Scalability** | 15% | System architecture, data pipeline, and ability to scale beyond a demo environment 

 |
| **5. Presentation & Demo** | 15% | Clarity of communication and quality of the working demonstration or prototype 

 |

 |
